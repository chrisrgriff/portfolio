A user-centered and data driven process

Christopher's approach to design projects is one rooted in research and analytics and has been crafted over many months of practice. It is detailed below and has been employed during work on a number of different projects.

During Christopher's time as a part of the most recent cohort of the MadUX Capstone Certificate in User Experience Design through the University of Wisconsin-Madison, he has outlined and refined the process through which he tackles design problems.

Loosely based on the Design Thinking Process defined by the Interaction Design Foundation, Christopher's process starts and ends with the user in mind. After the definition of a design problem as determined through the use of data analysis, competitive reviews, and persona evaluation, he is able to make informed decisions about where time and effort should be spent to make improvements to an existing system.

Ideation and sketching formally kickoff the design phase, putting pen to paper to flesh out possible design solutions. This leads to wireframing and rapid prototyping, an iteratative approach to design in a format that lends itself to testing.

During the next phase, Christopher tries to capture as much information as possible through usability testing. The analysis of this newly collected data will help point to the definition of new problems as the process begins to repeat itself.
